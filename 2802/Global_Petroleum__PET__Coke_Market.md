 <p><strong>Petroleum Coke (PET Coke)</strong>: A carbon-rich solid material derived from the refining process of crude oil. It is primarily used as a fuel source in industrial applications, such as power generation and cement production, and as a feedstock in metallurgical processes. PET coke is categorized into two types: <strong>fuel-grade</strong> (used for energy production) and <strong>calcined coke</strong> (used in aluminum, steel, and titanium manufacturing). Its high carbon content makes it a cost-effective alternative to coal but raises environmental concerns due to its sulfur and heavy metal content.</p>
