 <p>Amorphous graphite is a naturally occurring seam mineral that forms from the geologic metamorphism of anthracite coal. Contrary to the name, ?amorphous? graphite is not truly amorphous, but is a microcrystalline form of natural graphite. The morphology of amorphous graphite is typically granular except in extremely fine grinds, which have a flakey structure.</p>
